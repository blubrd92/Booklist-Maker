Booklister for Libraries

Booklister is free for everyone. The tool at booklister.org is the full tool, no restrictions. If your library is interested in a branded instance with your own subdomain, logo, staff accounts, and admin delegation, read on.

What a branded instance looks like

Your library's branding from the start. Your name and logo become the default, so every booklist your staff creates starts on-brand without any manual setup.

Your own subdomain. Something like libraryname.booklister.org. Stable, easy to bookmark, and configured for your library specifically.

Staff accounts and admin delegation. A designated admin at your library adds and removes staff through a dedicated admin console without needing to go through me. You stay in control of your own roster.

Public-tool fallback. If the branded instance ever has an issue, your staff can fall back to the public tool at booklister.org. The core tool is the same; only the branding differs.

Catalog integrations

If your library wants Booklister to search its own catalog system instead of (or alongside) Open Library, that's possible but handled as a separate conversation. The complexity varies depending on the system and what you need. If this is something you're interested in, get in touch and we can talk through what it would involve.

What to expect

This is a side project around my day job, so I can't promise I'll be able to set up every library that asks. I'll do my best when time allows. Setup itself is usually straightforward once we're ready to go, but timelines depend on my availability. I work on bugs and improvements as I can, and anything I build goes into the main tool and rolls out to every instance.

Winding down

If your library ever decides to stop using a branded instance, send me a note from the contact page and I'll confirm in writing, then remove the configuration, staff memberships, and branding assets. Download anything you still want (saved .booklist files, exported PDFs) before the takedown. I aim to complete removal requests within a few days of confirmation.

Getting in touch

The best first step is a conversation. Head over to the contact page and send me a note. I'd rather talk through your library's actual workflow and needs than ask you to commit to anything up front.
