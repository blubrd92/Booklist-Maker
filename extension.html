Booklister Helper

Booklister Helper is a small, free browser extension that captures books straight from your library's online catalog into Booklister. Click an icon on a book's catalog page and the title, author, call number, and cover all land in your clipboard, ready to paste into Booklister's Quick Add. No retyping, no copy-paste juggling, no hunting for cover images.

Works with BiblioCommons-powered catalogs only. That covers most North American public-library consortiums (MARINet, Sonoma County, San Mateo County, Pima County, and many more), but not catalogs running on Aspen Discovery, Vega, Polaris LEAP, Encore, or Sierra. If you're not sure, look at any book's page on your catalog: if the URL contains bibliocommons.com, you're good.

What it does

Three workflows, depending on what you're doing:

Single book

  1. Open a book's record page in your BiblioCommons catalog (URL contains /v2/record/).
  2. Click the Booklister Helper icon in your browser toolbar.
  3. One TSV row lands on your clipboard. Paste into Booklister's Quick Add → Spreadsheet tab and hit Cmd/Ctrl+Enter.

Curated list with selection

  1. Open a BiblioCommons list page (URL contains /v2/list/): staff picks, themed reading lists, etc.
  2. Click the toolbar icon. A popup opens showing every book on the list with a cover thumbnail and a checkbox.
  3. Pick the 13 to 15 books you want. Use the All, First 13, or None buttons for quick presets.
  4. Click Capture N books. Takes about five to ten seconds for thirteen books while it fetches call numbers and covers.
  5. Multi-row TSV lands on your clipboard. Paste into Booklister and submit.

Running list mode

If you'd rather collect books one at a time as you browse, turn on Accumulate single-book captures into a running list in the extension's options. Then each toolbar click appends to a running list instead of overwriting the clipboard, and the toolbar badge shows how many books are staged. Paste into Booklister whenever you have enough. Right-click the toolbar icon and pick Clear accumulated list to start over.

Branch selection

Most BiblioCommons catalogs span a consortium with different call numbers at different branches. By default the extension uses BiblioCommons's own "your local branch" detection, which works automatically when you're signed in to your library account or browsing from your library's IP range. If you'd rather pin a specific branch (for outreach booklists, school visits, etc.), set the Preferred branch field in the extension's options to a substring of your branch name as it appears in the catalog's Availability table.

What it doesn't do

  • It doesn't send anything to me or to any server I control. The TSV goes from the catalog page straight to your clipboard, locally, in your browser.
  • It doesn't run on any site other than BiblioCommons. The extension has no permission to read other websites and won't see what you're browsing.
  • It doesn't track you, doesn't include analytics, and doesn't load any code from the internet. It ships as a fixed bundle of files and runs entirely offline once installed.
